The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It plays an important role in stabilizing Earth's rotation and creating ocean tides.

Key Facts

  • The Moon is about 4.5 billion years old.
  • It is around 384,400 km away from Earth.
  • The Moon has no atmosphere, which is why the sky looks black from its surface.

Importance

The Moon influences tides, helps regulate Earth's climate, and has inspired cultures, stories, and scientific exploration for centuries.

Interesting Fact

Did you know? The Moon always shows the same face to Earth because it rotates at the same speed as it orbits our planet!
